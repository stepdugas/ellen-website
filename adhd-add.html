Understanding ADHD

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects attention, focus, and impulse control. It is often misunderstood and can present differently across individuals. Coaching offers strategies tailored to each person's needs.

Common Challenges

People with ADHD often face difficulties with time management, organization, task initiation, and emotional regulation. These challenges can impact work, school, and personal relationships.

My Approach

I work with clients to identify their strengths, develop practical systems, and build routines that support their goals. Together, we focus on improving executive functioning in a compassionate and collaborative way.

Who Can Benefit

Coaching can be transformative for students, professionals, creatives, and anyone feeling overwhelmed by their ADHD symptoms. Whether newly diagnosed or managing for years, support is available.

What to Expect

Sessions are individualized, goal-oriented, and empowering. We may explore tools like planners, reminders, habit tracking, or mindfulness depending on what works for you. Our work together is supportive and nonjudgmental.
